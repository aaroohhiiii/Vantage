## GTM.md

### Exact target user
An engineering manager or CTO at a 10–50 person B2B SaaS company that raised a Seed or Series A in the last 18 months. They approved the Cursor and Claude subscriptions when the team was 5 people, and haven't revisited the spend since the team hit 20. They have a $500–$2,000/month AI tool budget they've never formally audited.

### What they Google or scroll through
- "is cursor pro worth it for teams"
- "claude team vs api direct"
- "ai tools cost engineering team"
- They scroll: Hacker News (especially Show HN and Ask HN), X/Twitter engineering accounts they follow, Lenny's Newsletter, The Pragmatic Engineer

### Where they hang out online
- Hacker News — Show HN and Ask HN threads about developer tools
- r/ExperiencedDevs and r/cscareerquestions (read-only mostly)
- Indie Hackers (for founder side of this)
- Slack communities: Lenny's Slack, SaaStr Slack, specific YC alumni Slacks
- Twitter lists of "startup CTOs" and "engineering leads"

### How to get first 100 users in 30 days, $0 budget

Day 1–3: 
- Post "Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your AI tool spend" — write the exact HN title and first comment you'd post. The first comment should acknowledge limitations, invite criticism, and be humble.
- Post in r/ExperiencedDevs: "I audited my AI tool spend and saved $X — here's what I found" as a real story post with the tool at the end.

Day 4–7:
- DM 30 startup CTOs on Twitter who have tweeted about AI tools in the last 30 days. Personalized 2-line message referencing something they said.
- Post in Indie Hackers: "How much is your team spending on AI tools?" as a discussion thread. Drop the tool link naturally in a reply.

Day 8–14:
- Reach out to 5 AI tool newsletters (The Rundown AI, TLDR AI, Ben's Bites, The Neuron, Superhuman AI) with a one-paragraph pitch about why their readers would want this.
- Post the audit results of a "typical" team (real or anonymized) on Twitter as a thread: "I audited the AI tool spend of a 15-person startup. Here's what I found 🧵"

### Unfair distribution channel
Credex already has existing customers who buy AI credits. They can email their own customer list: "We built a free tool that shows you exactly where you're overspending — here's your free audit." Warm audience, zero CAC, high trust. This is something only Credex can do.

### Week 1 traction if this works
- 500+ page views from HN post
- 150+ audits completed (30% conversion from view to audit start)
- 40+ email captures (26% of completions)
- 5–8 Credex consultation bookings
- 3+ people sharing the tool organically on Twitter
